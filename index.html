Background — Sage

Raylock spent years cataloguing forbidden texts in an ancient library before his patron made contact through the margins of a crumbling tome. He still smells faintly of old parchment and regret.

Personality Traits

I use polysyllabic words that convey the impression of great erudition. I'm used to helping out those who aren't as smart as I am, and I do so in a way that makes them feel slow.

Ideals

Knowledge. The path to power and self-improvement is through knowledge. No price is too high for the right information.

Bonds

My patron gave me power when I was powerless. I owe it everything — even if I'm not entirely sure what it wants in return.

Flaws

I speak without thinking and regularly insult people without even noticing. I also have an obsessive need to understand things I cannot explain.

Feature — Researcher

When you attempt to learn or recall lore, if you don't know the information, you usually know where and from whom you can obtain it — a library, a sage, a forbidden text.

Description

Raylock is tall and lean with the typical infernal features of a Tiefling — small curved horns, a pointed tail, and solid golden eyes that seem to absorb light rather than reflect it. He dresses in dark scholarly robes and carries a black leather satchel stuffed with notes and loose pages.

Session Notes

The Arcane Collective

A loose network of scholars and mages Raylock once studied with. Relations are strained since his pact became known — some fear him, others want to study him.

Mirabel Ashcroft

A halfling cleric of Ioun. She doesn't trust Raylock's patron but trusts Raylock himself — for now. Helped him escape a bounty hunter last spring.

Inquisitor Valdris

A zealot witch-hunter who believes all warlock pacts are inherently corrupt. He has Raylock's name on a wanted list. Last seen in the city of Thornwall.

Origin

Raylock grew up in a mid-sized city as the son of a disgraced nobleman and a tiefling scholar. His mixed heritage made him an outsider in both worlds. He found solace in libraries, particularly in a restricted archive beneath the city's university.

The Pact

At age 23, Raylock discovered a tome written in a language that didn't exist — yet he could read it perfectly. Over the following weeks, words began appearing in the margins responding to his own notes. By the time he realized what was happening, the pact was already complete.

Present Day

Raylock travels as a freelance researcher and occasional problem-solver, using his eldritch powers carefully and his intellect constantly. He still doesn't know exactly what his patron wants.
